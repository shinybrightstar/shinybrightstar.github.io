<h1>From process engineering to data pipelines</h1>

<p>
I started out in chemical engineering — thinking in inputs, transformations, and outputs, and in systems
that have to keep running reliably. That process-thinking is what pulled me toward data engineering:
a pipeline is a process, a schema is a system, and both reward the same discipline.
</p>

<p>
Along the way I picked up an MBA, taught mathematics, worked in banking customer service, and spent time
as a Community Manager for GitHub's developer community — where I first started working seriously with
data to track engagement and growth. That role is where "I like measurable, verifiable outputs" stopped
being a preference and became a working principle.
</p>

<p>
Today I work as a Support Specialist while building toward data engineering full time: SQL (window
functions, CTEs, performance/indexing), pipeline orchestration, cloud data platforms, and warehouse design.
I write up everything I build — queries included, not just results — because I'd rather show my work than
describe it.
</p>

<p>
I'm based in Nigeria and looking for genuinely remote, globally-hireable data roles.
</p>
